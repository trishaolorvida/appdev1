Assignment 1: Exploring Emerging Technologies

1. Artificial Intelligence
Purpose: The primary goal of this technology is to make the workload easy for the education sector and the IT industry. 
It can help with the repetitive tasks and can lessen the workload.

Usage: We know that AI has been improving a lot these days so it can be useful to the education sector and the IT industry
in a way that AI can be used to calculate the students' grades quickly and efficiently, or use it for data analytics on the IT side.

Example: Education Sector - Gradescope is an AI-powered platform used by universities to automate the grading of assignments and exams.
Instructors save significant time on grading, allowing them to focus more on teaching and providing personalized feedback.

IT Industry - Google Analytics with AI Insights provides insights and recommendations to website owners. The owners of websites can 
use data-based choices to make enhancements in user engagement, optimization of marketing promotions, and improvement of customers’ 
interaction resulting in an increased business performance.


2. Internet of Things
Purpose:  Its primary goal is to create self-reporting devices that can communicate with each other (and users) in real time.
IoT describes the network of physical objects that are embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies for 
the purpose of connecting and exchanging data with other devices and systems over the internet.

Usage: IoT has a lot of usage and benefits to both sectors such as the creation of smart classrooms where devices like 
interactive whiteboards, smart projectors, and connected tablets are integrated to enhance the learning experience for
the education sector. Meanwhile, IT companies are heavily involved in developing IoT solutions for smart cities, where 
connected devices are used to manage urban infrastructure, including traffic lights, waste management, and energy grids.

Example: Education Sector - Georgia State University implemented IoT technology in its classrooms to create a more interactive 
and efficient learning environment. The university installed smart sensors and connected devices in classrooms to automate lighting, 
control temperature, and manage AV equipment. This has led to significant energy savings, more comfortable learning environments, 
and improved resource management.

IT Industry - Barcelona has implemented a variety of IoT solutions to create a smart city. This includes smart street lighting that 
adjusts based on pedestrian and vehicle traffic, IoT-enabled waste management systems that monitor trash levels and optimize collection 
routes, and connected public transportation systems that provide real-time updates to commuters.

3. 5G Technology
Purpose: It is meant to deliver higher multi-Gbps peak data speeds, ultra low latency, more reliability, massive network capacity, increased 
availability, and a more uniform user experience to more users.

Usage: For the Education Sector, it greatly enhance the connectivity of educational institutions, allowing for faster and more reliable internet access. 
This will enable more effective online learning, virtual classrooms, and collaboration among students and educators. As for the IT industry, is a 
game-changer, providing the necessary bandwidth and low latency required for innovations like autonomous vehicles, remote surgeries, 
and enhanced mobile experiences. It also facilitates the expansion of IoT networks and real-time data processing.

Example: Education Sector - During the COVID-19 pandemic, China leveraged 5G technology to enhance distance learning. 5G allowed for the seamless 
streaming of high-definition video lessons and enabled interactive virtual classrooms. 5G technology provided a reliable and high-quality learning 
experience for students, helping them stay engaged and connected to their education during the pandemic.

IT Industry - Verizon has used 5G technology to support autonomous drones for IT infrastructure inspection and management. The use of 5G-enabled 
drones has significantly reduced the time and cost associated with inspecting and maintaining IT infrastructure.


4. Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR)
Purpose: Augmented reality (AR) augments your surroundings by adding digital elements to a live view, often by using the camera on a smartphone. 
Virtual reality (VR) is a completely immersive experience that replaces a real-life environment with a simulated one.

Usage: VR and AR are used to create immersive educational environments where students can explore and interact with content in ways that are not 
possible in traditional classrooms. While, VR and AR are used in the IT industry for virtual prototyping and design, allowing engineers and designers 
to visualize and interact with their creations in a virtual space before they are built.

Example: Education Sector -  The University of Maryland uses VR to create a virtual chemistry lab where students can conduct experiments in a safe, 
controlled environment. This allows students to gain hands-on experience and better understand complex scientific concepts.

IT Industry - Ford uses VR in its design studio to create virtual prototypes of new vehicles. Designers can walk around the car, look inside, 
and make real-time adjustments to the design. VR enables more efficient and cost-effective design processes, reducing the need for physical 
prototypes and speeding up the development cycle.


5. Blockchain Technology
Purpose:  It is primarily used to create a secure and transparent system for recording and verifying data.

Usage: Blockchain technology has the potential to revolutionize the education sector by providing a secure, transparent, and efficient 
way to manage educational records and credentials. Meanwhile, it has significant applications in the IT industry, particularly in areas 
related to security, data management, and supply chain management.

Example: Education Sector - Verifying the authenticity of educational credentials, especially from institutions in different countries, 
can be time-consuming and prone to fraud. A blockchain-based platform can securely store student records, including diplomas, transcripts, 
and certificates. Each credential is assigned a unique hash value, making it tamper-proof.

IT Industry - Software supply chains can be vulnerable to attacks, such as malware injection or code tampering. Blockchain can be used to 
create an immutable record of the software development process, from the initial code to the final product. Each stage of the development 
process is recorded on the blockchain, including changes made to the code, the identities of developers involved, and the verification of code integrity.
